    • I'm working on the design and evaluation of a tool called Privacy Label Wiz (see publication), developed under the Usable Privacy Policy Project at Carnegie Mellon University. The purpose of the tool is to help iOS developers generate privacy labels that they are required to complete when they submit their application to the Apple App Store.

    For our Engineering Privacy in Software class at Carnegie Mellon, classmates and I developed a browser extension (see code) to help individuals exercise their rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). We call our extension "Nutcracker" since it cracks the nut of websites that hold privacy resources behind a series of pages that are possibly difficult to navigate.
